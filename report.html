Introduction

This analysis focuses on cognitive declinerelated health indicators from the CDC Alzheimers Disease and Healthy Aging Data. We examine percentages of older adults reporting subjective cognitive decline (SCD) and its impacts, using data from 20152022 across U.S. states. Our objective is to summarize these indicators and visualize their trends over time.

The dataset includes survey-based indicators of subjective cognitive decline (SCD) among older adults. We focus on four specific survey questions directly measuring SCD and its consequences.

The figure shows trends of subjective cognitive decline (SCD) indicators among older adults from 2015 to 2022. Overall, the percentages have remained relatively stable over time, with most indicators fluctuating only slightly. Indicators related to daily life interference and worsening memory symptoms consistently show higher prevalence (around 3545%), while the indicator reflecting the need for assistance remains much lower (about 1015%). This suggests that although many older adults experience cognitive decline symptoms, only a smaller proportion report needing help with day-to-day activities.
